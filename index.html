    About Website

    This website is created as a digital showcase of my undergraduate research project titled "An Implementation of SIDH using Maple." It serves as a comprehensive platform where visitors can explore the technical details, goals, and outcomes of the project in an accessible format. The website not only highlights the implementation of the SIDH (Supersingular Isogeny Diffie–Hellman) cryptographic protocol but also aims to educate and inform others about its significance in the field of post-quantum cryptography. It is designed with clarity and ease of navigation in mind, ensuring that both technical and non-technical audiences can benefit from its content.

    In addition to technical documentation, the website includes background context, code demonstrations, and visual representations that bring the mathematics behind SIDH to life. Visitors will also find information about the people behind the project, including a personal introduction and acknowledgment of mentorship. This platform is more than just a summary of work—it reflects a learning journey, a contribution to future-ready cryptography, and a place to engage with others who share an interest in secure, post-quantum systems.
